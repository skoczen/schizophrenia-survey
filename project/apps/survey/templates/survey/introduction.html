Introduction

Hello. I am Dr. Chavez. I'm going to introduce you to some patients of mine who have schizophrenia. I am doing this to understand how you see the problems caused by schizophrenia. We want your opinion about how good or bad it might be to live with certain symptoms. There are no right or wrong answers. Many people believe that schizophrenia means "split personality" but this isn't true. Schizophrenia is a severe mental illness caused by an imbalance of the chemicals in the brain. This affects how people see reality, how they relate to others, and how they conduct everyday tasks.

There are three types of symptoms commonly seen in schizophrenia. These are called positive, negative, and cognitive. Positive symptoms are psychological problems that have been added as a result of the illness. These can include hearing voices or seeing visions. A person with positive symptoms might hear a “special message”, just for him, on the radio or he might believe that he is being followed by the FBI. Negative symptoms are the result of what a person has lost because of schizophrenia. Someone with negative symptoms has difficulty reaching out and relating to other people. They may seem withdrawn or distant and their emotions are flat. They may speak and behave like a machine or robot. The third type of symptom is seen in a person's cognitive ability, and by that I mean the ability to think or reason. A person can be so confused by a whirlwind of thoughts that they can't finish a sentence. They might string together loosely connected ideas, and they may appear to lack common sense. For example, when asked what a proverb means, such as “people who live in glass houses shouldn’t throw stones,” a person with schizophrenia might respond with “throwing stones is bad”, which would be missing the point.

It can be difficult for someone with any of these symptoms to function in society. Schizophrenia typically strikes young men in their late teens and women in their late twenties. It's one of the most disabling illnesses faced by young adults. Fortunately, there are a number of medications that can help. The purpose of this survey is to learn how you would evaluate the symptoms of schizophrenia.
